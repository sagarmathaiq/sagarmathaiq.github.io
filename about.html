About Sagarmatha Analytics

Sagarmatha Analytics is a quantitative research initiative specializing in electoral forecasting and applied data science. Our team comprises Nepali professionals with advanced training in Physics, Mathematics, and statistical modeling, computational methods, and quantitative analysis.

We develop probabilistic forecasting frameworks that leverage Bayesian inference, Monte Carlo simulation, and game-theoretic principles to model electoral competition. Our methodological approach is grounded in peer-reviewed statistical techniques adapted to the unique challenges of forecasting in data-sparse environments.

Named after Mount Everest (Sagarmatha in Nepali), our mission is to advance the science of electoral forecasting in South Asia through transparent, reproducible methodologies that appropriately quantify uncertainty rather than projecting false precision.

Research Methodology

Our forecasting framework treats parliamentary elections as constrained optimization problems within a zero-sum competitive structure. Unlike conventional polling aggregation methods, we model the electorate as a fixed resource where vote gains by one candidate necessarily correspond to losses by others as a fundamental constraint.

We employ hierarchical modeling techniques that decompose candidate strength into constituent components: (1) historical party support adjusted for demographic shifts, (2) candidate-specific effects including incumbency advantage and celebrity status, and (3) stochastic consolidation dynamics among minor party voters. Monte Carlo methods generate probability distributions over electoral outcomes while maintaining zero-sum consistency across all simulations.

Nepal 2082: Methodological Challenges

The 2082 parliamentary elections present several analytical challenges that require novel modeling approaches:

  • Structural volatility: Emergence of new political parties (RSP) with limited historical data
  • Celebrity candidacies: High-profile candidates (Balen Shah, Gagan Kumar Thapa) with non-traditional appeal
  • Generational realignment: Youth mobilization creating potential for non-linear voter behavior
  • Coalition uncertainty: Complex multi-party consolidation scenarios requiring probability weighting

These factors necessitate forecasting models that can incorporate expert judgment on candidate strength while maintaining statistical rigor through probabilistic uncertainty quantification.

Technical Approach

Our model architecture consists of four primary components:

  • Base vote estimation: Historical party support scaled by voter registration growth and expected turnout
  • Strength multipliers: Parameterized adjustments for celebrity appeal, local connections, party organization, and demographic factors
  • Personal vote premium: Individual candidate effects derived from historical FPTP-PR differentials
  • Zero-sum normalization: Vote allocation ensuring aggregate predictions equal expected turnout

Model parameters are calibrated using historical election data, expert assessments, and constituency-specific characteristics. Uncertainty is propagated through Monte Carlo simulation (n=1,000) with configurable variance parameters reflecting forecast confidence.

Transparency and Limitations

We acknowledge several key limitations in our approach: (1) parameter estimates incorporate subjective expert judgment in the absence of high-quality polling data, (2) consolidation scenario probabilities are based on political analyst assessments rather than empirical measurement, and (3) the model assumes independent voter behavior within scenarios, potentially understating correlated uncertainty.

All forecasts are presented as probability distributions rather than point predictions, with explicit confidence levels provided. We encourage critical evaluation of our methodology and welcome engagement from the research community.
